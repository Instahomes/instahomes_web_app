Top Indicators of A Valuable Property

What do you need to find good homes in the Philippines? Read about how to find trustworthy & valuable properties through these key indicators – InstaHomes.

Looking for the perfect property may seem daunting because of all the factors to consider. You need to keep up with the current market trends, be familiar with the terms, and take into consideration your personal preferences. In this article, we're easing the burden by presenting the indicators that you need to get started on your search for the perfect property.

Location Indicators

The three words of advice that you hear the most in real estate are "Location, Location, Location." Buying a good property involves looking for the best location that would suit your preferences and purpose. This also ensures getting the most value for your investment.

The place where a property is situated in tells a lot about the lifestyle that the surrounding communities have. If you're looking to buy a home for you and your family to relax, wind down, or be away from the bustling noise of the city, then it would probably be a good choice to look at provincial properties.

Preferences may differ per person. Once you settle on your purpose for investment, here are three important aspects that should help you find the right location for your new home.

1. Accessibility

We all know that we shouldn't just buy a property in the middle of nowhere, but how do we know which locations are valuable? We can start with its accessibility. Can you get there easily through different means of transportation (e.g. by car or by commute)? Are there any nearby facilities that you can quickly go to when needed (e.g. hospitals, convenience stores, malls, etc.)? How long will it take to get to your day-to-day activities (e.g. work, school)?

2. Safety

With accessibility also comes the importance of safety. In buying a good home, you have to safeguard yourself and your entire family. Some questions that you could think about in terms of the location's security could be: Am I comfortable to walk around the area alone? Would I let my children go out to play in front of the house? When looking at villages, you can also check how the guards in that particular subdivision treat you as a visitor.

3. Amenities

Finally, it is also viable to think about the amenities that you would frequent, depending on your lifestyle. How near are you to business centers like malls and restaurants, parks, areas of worship, and other places of interest?

Market Trends

Once you decide on your location or area, a good indicator of a valuable property and investment comes from the market trends of that place. It's good to be able to compare these rates with other areas that you may also be interested in, as well as with popular markets in order to have a benchmark. Here are two important trends to monitor:

1. Vacancy Rate

This lets you know about the demand for rental properties in the area. A higher vacancy rate shows lower demand for properties, meaning not a lot of people are investing in this market. Therefore, there are more properties that are cheaper to own. On the other hand, a lower vacancy rate shows that the area has more competitive properties. You may want to look into the possible reasons for such, but all in all, this measurement can also show you the difference in prices within the markets.

2. Days on Market (DOM)

In relation to the vacancy rate, checking out the average DOM trend would also be a good indicator of activity in the area. If the trend is going upward, then that means the properties have been available for a longer time, and there would be a higher opportunity to close a deal.

Supply and Demand

As with any other investment, a crucial aspect to consider in terms of finding the right place and the right property would boil down to its economic indicators — or supply and demand. Whether its in a Central Business District (CBD) or in a more laid-back location, economic activity and opportunities available around the property will show great support for your investment.
