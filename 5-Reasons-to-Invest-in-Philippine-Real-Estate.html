5 Reasons to Invest in Philippine Real Estate

Why invest in real estate in the Philippines right now? We present 5 compelling reasons in our guide to real estate investing in the Philippines.

Share on:  

1. Real Estate Market Resilience

The real estate industry in the Philippines is known for its resilience relative to other real estate markets.

One reason for this is that the Central Bank of the Philippines (BSP) limits the real estate loans given by banks. Historically, this meant that while other real estate markets like that of Thailand have previously crashed during real estate bubbles, the Philippines’ market remained afloat as real estate developers avoided bankruptcy.

Another reason is due to Overseas Filipino Worker (OFW) remittances of which over 50% is invested into Real Estate. Even during the pandemic, OFW remittances helped maintain demand as remittances and subsequent investments only dropped by 0.8% according to Cushman & Wakefield — much lower than expected.

2. Post-Pandemic Market Outlook

Despite taking a hit during the pandemic, the market is expected to rise once again. According to Colliers Philippines, the recovery in demand is likely due to low mortgage rates and increasingly attractive payment terms from developers.

This rise in demand is especially true for urban areas outside of Metro Manila such as Lipa City in Batangas due to the expected infrastructure projects, preference for less crowded areas, and the prevalence of work-from-home setups driving queries and sales of properties.

While prices dropped in 2020, JLL expects both rent and selling prices to start picking up again by the second half of 2021.

3. Cheaper to Own Than the Other Countries

There are many internationally competitive properties one can buy in the Philippines at better deals than abroad. For one, due to the pandemic developers are offering special deals with much cheaper prices for condominiums and homes to maintain their sales. Another reason is the lower interest rates for real estate investing due to policy changes by the BSP to increase sales in the industry.

Finally, the best deals can also be found in properties in urban areas outside of Metro Manila such as Lipa City. While they are currently very affordable, increased demand from both domestic and foreign investors mean they are likely to rise in price and rent charge in the coming years.

4. Passive Income and Equity Gains

Prior to the pandemic the Philippines was in a “decade long property boom.” Driven by rapid economic growth, OFW remittances, tourism, and foreign investments, property prices as well as rental prices rose consistently and considerably. Before the COVID pandemic, rent prices rose and were expected to keep rising by 8% annually until 2021. This allowed property owners to earn passive income from rentals while also earning property gains from the increasing cost.

Given this, in the long term the market is expected to return to this state. While the market faced some setbacks due to the pandemic, in the long run BPO investments and investments from other foreign companies coming into the country are expected to return as well as tourism. This will directly benefit commercial and office space markets, but also indirectly affect residential units — leading to the same price and rental appreciation enjoyed previously.
